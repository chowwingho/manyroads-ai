06 — PRINCIPLES

Design Principles

Quick reference for decision-making when extending the system.

Restraint over decoration
Every element earns its place. No gradients, illustrations, or stock photography.
Typography-forward
Type does the heavy lifting. Bold headlines, generous spacing, clear hierarchy.
Warm, not cold
Creams and warm grays, not blue-gray-white. We're approachable engineers, not a corporate consultancy.
Dark mode as first-class
Not an afterthought. Both modes are fully designed with intentional contrast.
Developer familiarity
Monospace accents, underscore conventions, minimal chrome. Our audience writes code — the interface should feel native to them.
✓ This looks like us
Bold left-aligned headlines. Warm cream backgrounds. Monospace section markers with underscores. Cards with generous padding and subtle borders. No hero images. Stats that speak for themselves. A footer that's always dark.
✗ This doesn't look like us
Centered text. Blue/purple gradients. Stock photos of people at whiteboards. Rounded pill buttons in bright colors. Animated hero illustrations. "Book a demo" in a floating modal. Anything that feels like every other SaaS landing page.
